History of National Space Day

National Space Day in India is a celebration of the country's remarkable achievements in space exploration.India became the fourth country to land on the moon and the first to reach its southern polar region on August 23, 2023. To honour this landmark achievement, Hon'ble Prime Minister Shri Narendra Modi announced August 23 as "National Space Day". This landmark event marked India's entry into the space race and set the foundation for future missions.

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) has since become a global leader in space technology, with numerous successful missions, including the Chandrayaan and Mangalyaan programs. National Space Day honors the visionaries, scientists, and engineers who have contributed to India's space program, making the nation a proud member of the global space community.
